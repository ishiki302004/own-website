My Top 5 Favorite Songs

1. Mirrorball by Taylor Swift



Taylor Swift's song "Mirrorball" depicts her as a mirror ball, a mirror-like object that is commonly suspended atop a dance floor at discos to reflect light and create an exciting atmosphere. The singer, like this object, admits that instead of being herself, she seeks to understand and do things to please others.

2. Kill this Love by Blackpink



Lyrically, the song recounts the decision of all members to terminate unhealthy romantic relationships, to stand your ground and recognize your worth, and to eventually "kill" any love that is not positive.

3. Hard Feelings/Loveless by Lorde



"Hard Feelings/Loveless" could be about Lorde's breakup with her ex-boyfriend in 2015. Its two halves contrast both sonically and thematically. "Hard Feelings," the first section of the song, is a tranquil, dreamy music that describes the emotions that followed the divorce and the ultimate healing from heartbreak.

4. Supercut by Lorde



"Supercut" is a sorrowful, emotional song about feeling confused after a romantic relationship fails. According to fan beliefs, the song is about her split with a boyfriend in 2015, and the song itself implies that the relationship wasn't really going anywhere and that Lorde didn't know how to heal it. So, in this song, she reflects on only the happy times, tries to forget the negative, and wonders what went wrong.

5. Lovesick Girls by Blackpink



"Lovesick Girls" is an EDM and dance-pop song about post-breakup sorrow and acute heartbreak, as well as why they feel the urge to still look for love while believing they were "born to be alone."
